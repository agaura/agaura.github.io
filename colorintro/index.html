Color: A Linguistic View

Posted at — Sep 09, 2022

Introduction

   Color is a fundamental experience shared by all those who’re able to see, and in our languages we’ve developed many ways to talk about it. However, words are imprecise, and unfortunately for the word color, it is a term that is used by quite a decent number of fields while having a different meaning in each of them. When a physicist talkes about color, do they mean the same thing as an artist does? And what about when you get complimented on the color of your shirt by a friend? The fact of the matter is that these people are not really talking about the exact same thing.

   In particular, this post is especially designed to address those “pink isn’t a real color!” or “brown isn’t a real color!” statements. Frankly, I’m never a fan of appeals to a “higher” discipline, like physics 😖 (Not that there’s anything wrong with physics, but it gets too much importance). Instead, I wanna take a linguistic and sociological approach: see how people be using the word color, and how we can better understand the phenomena itself by examining all the different ways people use it.
